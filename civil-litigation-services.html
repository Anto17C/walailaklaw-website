Expert civil litigation services for foreigners

Civil litigation involves legal disputes between individuals or organisations relating to money, property, contracts, or personal rights. Whether you're facing a contractual dispute, property conflict, inheritance matter, or financial claim, our firm provides clear legal advice and structured representation throughout the Thai civil court process.

Common civil litigation disputes we handle

  • Contract disputes — breach of contract and service agreement disputes
  • Property & real estate disputes — land ownership conflicts, boundary disputes
  • Family law & estate matters — inheritance disputes, probate of wills
  • Torts & negligence — personal injury and financial loss claims
Process

Our civil litigation process

The 4-step process for civil cases in Thailand.

1

Initial legal consultation

We analyze your dispute to determine the strength of your case and the potential remedies available under Thai law.

2

Mediation & negotiation

Before a full trial, Thai courts strongly encourage mediation. We represent you during these negotiations to reach a settlement quickly and cost-effectively.

3

Filing the complaint (Plaint)

If a settlement is not possible, we draft and file a formal complaint with the appropriate court, ensuring all documentation is translated and authenticated.

4

Trial & judgment

Our litigators provide robust representation in court, presenting evidence and examining witnesses to secure a favorable verdict and subsequent enforcement.

Why choose Walailak Law for civil cases?

Choosing the right legal representation in a civil dispute is critical, as outcomes can significantly affect your financial position and legal rights. We prioritise negotiation and mediation where appropriate; when litigation is necessary, our lawyers provide structured and strategic court representation under the Thai Civil and Commercial Code.
