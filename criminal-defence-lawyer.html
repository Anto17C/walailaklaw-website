Right to counsel — the 48-hour rule

Under Thai law, authorities may detain an alleged offender for up to 48 hours for investigation. If further time is required, the police must obtain a court order. During this window, you're entitled to:

  • Right to information — be informed of charges and shown a valid arrest warrant
  • Right to counsel — privately consult a lawyer present at every step
  • Right to silence — remain silent or provide testimony at your discretion
  • Language support — a court-appointed or police-provided interpreter
