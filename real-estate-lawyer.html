Real estate lawyer for foreigners in Thailand

Property transactions in Thailand require careful legal review, particularly for foreign investors and expatriates. We assist clients with due diligence, title verification, sales and purchase agreements, condominium acquisitions, leasehold structures, and registration at the Land Office.

Our objective is to protect your ownership rights and reduce legal risk in high-value property transactions.

Foreign ownership & legal structures

Under Thai law, foreign nationals are generally restricted from owning land freehold. Lawful structures are available, including condominium ownership within the foreign quota and long-term lease agreements. We advise on structuring acquisitions to protect your long-term investment interests.

Common questions about buying property in Thailand

While foreigners generally cannot own land freehold, they can own the building (house) itself and secure the land through a long-term 30-year leasehold (renewable) or a Usufruct agreement. Alternatively, some investors use a Thai Limited Company for business-related land use. Our real estate lawyers help you choose the safest legal structure.
A Chanote is the strongest form of Thai land title, confirming exact boundaries surveyed by satellite/GPS. Other titles (such as Nor Sor 3 Gor) carry more restrictions and less certainty over exact boundaries.
Costs typically include transfer fees, stamp duty or specific business tax, and withholding tax, which vary depending on the property value and ownership duration. We can estimate these costs during due diligence.
