The Importance Of Property Due Diligence

Before any deposit is paid, we conduct structured due diligence to identify legal risks. This includes:

  • Verification of legal ownership and chain of title
  • Confirmation of zoning and land-use restrictions
  • Review of building permits and construction compliance
  • Litigation and encumbrance search

Early legal review reduces the risk of defective title, hidden liabilities, and unenforceable contracts.

Foreign Ownership And Legal Structures

Under Thai law, foreign nationals are generally restricted from owning land freehold. However, lawful structures are available, including condominium ownership within the foreign quota and long-term lease agreements.

Our real estate lawyer provides legal advice on structuring property acquisitions to ensure compliance with Thai regulations while protecting your long-term investment interests.

Common Questions

While foreigners generally cannot own land freehold, they can own the building (house) itself and secure the land through a long-term 30-year leasehold (renewable) or a leasehold agreement. Alternatively, some investors use a Thai Limited Company for business-related land use. Our real estate lawyers help you choose the safest legal structure.
A Chanote (Nor Sor 4 Jor) is the highest form of land title in Thailand, providing full ownership rights with accurate GPS surveying. Other titles such as Nor Sor 3 Gor offer fewer rights and carry greater risk. We always recommend purchasing only Chanote-titled land for security.
Costs typically include Transfer Fee (2% of appraised value), Specific Business Tax (3.3%) or Stamp Duty (0.5% if held over 5 years), and Withholding Tax. Both buyer and seller may share these costs depending on the agreement. We calculate and advise on all costs before you proceed.
