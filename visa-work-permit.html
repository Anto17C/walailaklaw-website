Securing Your Legal Stay And Right To Work In Thailand With Precision And Compliance

Navigating Thai immigration and labour regulations can be complex, especially for foreigners relocating to Thailand for business, employment, family, retirement, or long-term residence. Visa and work permit applications in Thailand require accurate documentation, careful planning, and compliance with multiple authorities, including the Thai Immigration Bureau, Ministry of Labour, and related government offices.

Our expert visa lawyer assists clients in Rayong, Pattaya, Chonburi and Bangkok with end-to-end visa and work permit services, helping individuals, business owners, investors, and employers obtain the proper legal status to live and work in Thailand with confidence.

Choosing The Right Visa Category

Selecting the correct visa is the first and most important step. A foreigner entering Thailand for business, work, or employment purposes must use the proper immigration route from the beginning. A person cannot legally work in Thailand with only a tourist visa or without the appropriate supporting documents.

Our legal team and experienced lawyers advise clients in Rayong, Pattaya, Chonburi and Bangkok on the most suitable visa category based on their purpose of stay, employment structure, family situation, and long-term plans. This helps prevent unnecessary delays, rejected applications, or immigration compliance issues later on.

Work Permit Requirements In Thailand

A work permit is generally required for any foreign national legally working in Thailand. The authorities review not only the individual's qualifications, but also the sponsoring company's registration, paid-up capital, office location, and Thai employee ratio.

For many businesses, the common benchmark is 2 million THB in registered capital per foreign work permit, together with the required ratio of Thai employees to foreign staff. Because these requirements are closely linked to company structure, our firm coordinates both corporate registration and immigration compliance to put your business on the right legal foundation.

Employer And Corporate Compliance

For foreign employees and directors, a successful visa or work permit application often depends on whether the sponsoring company is fully compliant. Authorities may review company registration status, paid-up capital, company objectives, office and supporting lease documents, Tax and VAT registration, employment contracts, and Thai staff roster and supporting base documents.

Our experienced lawyers help employers and foreign investors in Rayong, Pattaya, and Bangkok align their corporate structure with immigration and labour requirements, reducing the risk of refusal or future compliance issues.
