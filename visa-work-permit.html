Navigating Thai immigration and labour regulations

Visa and work permit applications require accurate documentation, careful planning, and compliance with multiple authorities, including the Thai Immigration Bureau and Ministry of Labour. Our expert visa lawyer assists individuals, business owners, and employers in obtaining proper legal status to live and work in Thailand with confidence.

Visa types & work permit services

  • Non-Immigrant "B" Visa for business owners, directors, and foreign employees
  • Work permit applications for foreigners legally employed by Thai companies
  • Business visa renewals and extensions
  • Dependent and family visa support
  • Retirement and marriage visa assistance
  • 90-day reporting and immigration compliance support

Work permit requirements

A work permit is generally required for any foreign national engaging in work in Thailand. Thai authorities review the sponsoring company's registration, paid-up capital, tax compliance, and Thai employee ratio. A common benchmark is 2 million THB in registered capital per foreign work permit, alongside required Thai staff ratios.

Renewals, extensions & ongoing obligations

Obtaining the initial visa or work permit is only the first step. Foreign nationals must continue to comply with:

  • Annual extension applications and work permit renewal
  • 90-day reporting and address updates after employment changes
  • Re-entry permits before leaving Thailand
